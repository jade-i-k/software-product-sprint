I have been intrigued by other languages for as long as I can remember. The first time this interest has truly been able to be flourish, however, was when I got the opportunity to study Mandarin Chinese at my old high-school, the Spence School. Ever since then, I have absolutely loved taking the time to study the language and Chinese culture in general.

I am still taking Chinese at Columbia but I plan for this to be my last semester of it. This is due to the fact that past summer, when everyone was encouraged to stay inside and quarantine, I dedicated a lot of my time towards reviewing Chinese along with beginning to learn some Japanese on the side. Doing so made me realize I've reached a level of proficiency in Mandarin where I can focus on self-study and still significantly improve my fluency level. I really appreciated the fact I was able to structure what I learned around topics I found most relevant to my personal life along with set a more flexible schedule that accommodated any unknown difficulties I encountered.

Despite the fact that I won't be continuing to learn Mandarin in a formal setting during college, I'll always appreciate the memories I have made with my old teachers and classmates. My fondest memory was when I was able to visit China for two weeks with other students in my high-school who were all also taking the language. I've included some pictures I took below!
